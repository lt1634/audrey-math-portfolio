Stage 3: Problem Solving

Word Problems for 5-Year-Olds

1

Logical Thinking

Children develop the ability to think step-by-step and make connections between different pieces of information.

Example: 'If I have 3 apples and eat 1, how many are left?'
2

Simple Puzzles

Children learn to solve age-appropriate puzzles that require mathematical reasoning and problem-solving skills.

Example: Number sequence puzzles, pattern recognition games
3

Story-Based Math

Children solve mathematical problems embedded in engaging stories and real-life scenarios.

Example: 'The three little pigs need to share 12 apples equally'
4

Group Problem Solving

Children learn to work together, share ideas, and collaborate to solve mathematical challenges.

Example: Team building challenges, collaborative puzzle solving
5

Solution Explanation

Children learn to explain their thinking process and justify their solutions to mathematical problems.

Example: 'I counted 5 because I added 2 and 3 together'

STAGE 1: NUMBER RECOGNITION AND COUNTING (1-10)
=====================================

CONTENT OVERVIEW:
Here's engaging, age-appropriate content for 3-year-old children learning number recognition and counting (1-10) in a Hong Kong kindergarten context. The activities are designed to be hands-on, fun, and incorporate familiar local elements.

---

**Stage 1: Number Recognition and Counting (1-10)**

**Overall Learning Goals for 3-year-olds:**
*   To verbally count numbers from 1 to 10.
*   To count objects up to 5 (initially) with one-to-one correspondence.
*   To begin to recognize and match numerals 1-5 with their corresponding quantities. (Extension to 10 gradually).

---

### **Lesson 1: Discovering Numbers 1, 2, 3 â€“ "Dim Sum Delight!"**

**Learning Objectives:** Children will be able to:
*   Count up to 3 objects with one-to-one correspondence.
*   Recognize and match numerals 1, 2, 3 with quantities.
*   Recite numbers 1, 2, 3.

**Materials:**
*   Large, clear number cards (1, 2, 3).
*   Small, colorful plastic or foam *siu mai* (pork dumplings) or *har gao* (shrimp dumplings) â€“ 3 per child/small group.
*   Small plates for each child.
*   Picture cards showing 1, 2, or 3 Hong Kong-themed items (e.g., 1 Star Ferry, 2 double-decker buses, 3 goldfish).

**Activity 1: Warm-up Song & Finger Counting (5 minutes)**

*   **Teacher Script:** "Good morning, little stars! Let's get our counting fingers ready! Can you show me one finger? Yes, just ONE! How about two fingers? Wow! Let's sing our 'Counting with Fingers' song!"
*   **Song (Tune: "Are You Sleeping?"):**
    *   "One little finger, one little finger,
        One little finger, count with me!
        One, one, one, clap, clap, clap!
        One little finger, point to number one!"
    *   (Repeat for two, then three fingers, adjusting lyrics accordingly).
*   **Action:** Children hold up fingers as they count.

**Activity 2: Hands-on Counting â€“ "My Dim Sum Plate!" (10-12 minutes)**

*   **Teacher Script:** "Who loves yummy *dim sum*? Today, we have special *siu mai* to count! Each of you has a plate. Let's put some *siu mai* on our plates and count them carefully."
*   **Introduction to Number 1:**
    *   "Look! I have ONE *siu mai*! Can you put ONE *siu mai* on your plate? Say 'ONE' as you put it down. Let's see your ONE *siu mai*!" (Show number '1' card). "This is number ONE!"
*   **Introduction to Number 2:**
    *   "Now, let's add another one! One... two! We have TWO *siu mai*! Can you put TWO *siu mai* on your plate? Count them: 'ONE, TWO'!" (Show number '2' card). "This is number TWO!"
*   **Introduction to Number 3:**
    *   "Let's add one more! One... two... three! Wow, THREE *siu mai*! Can you put THREE *siu mai* on your plate? Count them: 'ONE, TWO, THREE'!" (Show number '3' card). "This is number THREE!"
*   **Reinforcement:** "Great job counting your *siu mai*! How many do you have now? Three!" (Gesture to show '3' with fingers).

**Activity 3: Matching Game â€“ "Number Card Fun!" (8-10 minutes)**

*   **Teacher Script:** "We've counted our *siu mai*! Now let's find the special number cards that match! Remember this number? (Show '1' card). What number is this? ONE! Can you find a picture with ONE thing?"
*   **Game Play:**
    1.  Place the number cards (1, 2, 3) on a mat or table.
    2.  Scatter the Hong Kong-themed picture cards (e.g., 1 Star Ferry, 2 double-decker buses, 3 goldfish).
    3.  Children pick a picture card, count the items on it, and then place it next to the corresponding number card.
    *   **Teacher:** "Who found the card with TWO double-decker buses? Yes! Which number card does it go with? Number TWO! Super!"
*   **Hands-on:** Children take turns, verbalizing the count and the number.

### **Progression and Extension to Numbers 4-10:**

*   **Gradual Introduction:** Introduce numbers 4, 5, then 6-10 in subsequent lessons, using the same activity structure. Do not introduce too many new numbers at once.
*   **Varied Manipulatives:**
    *   **Numbers 4-5:** Use Hong Kong-style egg tarts, pineapple buns, or *lai see* (red packets).
    *   **Numbers 6-10:** Use plastic fishballs on skewers (up to 10), small toy MTR trains, building blocks (to create a stack of 10), or counting beads.
*   **"Counting Circle":** Children sit in a circle. One child starts by saying "one," the next says "two," and so on, passing a plush toy (e.g., a panda) around the circle.
*   **"Number Scavenger Hunt":** Hide number cards (1-5, then 1-10) around the classroom. Children find a number card, identify the numeral, and then collect that many of a specific item (e.g., "Find number 4, then bring me 4 blue blocks").
*   **"Number Line Hop":** Create a large number line (1-10) on the floor using masking tape or large number cutouts. Call out a number, and children hop to it.
*   **"Clap, Tap, Stomp":** Call out a number (e.g., "Number 7!"). Children then do 7 claps, 7 taps on their knees, or 7 stomps.
*   **Role-Play:** Set up a pretend market stall (Hong Kong *Dai Pai Dong*). "How many fishballs do you want? Three? Here are ONE, TWO, THREE fishballs!"
*   **Stories & Rhymes:** Continuously use number-themed stories and songs that reinforce counting and number recognition up to 10.

---

**Educator Notes:**

*   **Repetition is Key:** Young children learn through repeated exposure and practice in various contexts.
*   **Sensory Engagement:** Encourage touching, feeling, and moving with the numbers.
*   **Observation:** Pay attention to children's individual progress. Some may grasp counting quickly, while others need more support with one-to-one correspondence or numeral recognition.
*   **Positive Reinforcement:** Celebrate every effort and correct answers with enthusiasm.
*   **Keep it Fun:** Learning math at this age should be a joyful and playful experience.

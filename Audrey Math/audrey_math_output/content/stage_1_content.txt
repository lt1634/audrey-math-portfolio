STAGE 1: NUMBER RECOGNITION AND COUNTING (1-10)
=====================================

CONTENT OVERVIEW:
Here is engaging, age-appropriate content for 3-year-old children learning number recognition and counting (1-10), specifically tailored for a Hong Kong kindergarten setting.

---

**Stage 1: Number Recognition and Counting (1-10)**

**Overall Objectives:**
*   Children will be able to recognize and name numerals 1-10.
*   Children will be able to count objects 1-10 with one-to-one correspondence.
*   Children will understand that the last number counted represents the total quantity.
*   Children will use simple counting in daily routines and play.

**General Strategies for All Numbers:**
*   **Repetition is Key:** Keep activities short, fun, and repeat them often.
*   **Multi-Sensory:** Use visuals, songs, touch, and movement.
*   **Real-World Connections:** Point out numbers and quantities in the classroom and daily life.
*   **Positive Reinforcement:** Encourage every attempt at counting and number recognition.

---

**Lesson Block 1: Focus on Numbers 1, 2, 3**

**Warm-up (5 minutes): "Counting Hands" Song**
*   **Teacher:** "Let's get our counting hands ready! (Wiggle fingers). Sing with me!"
*   **Song (Tune: "Twinkle, Twinkle Little Star"):**
    "One, two, three, let's count with me!
    Numbers are fun, you see!
    Clap your hands, one, two, three,
    Counting is fun for you and me!"
    (Repeat with actions like tapping knees, wiggling toes).

---

**Activity 1: Number 1 - "One and Only!"**

*   **Concept:** Understanding 'one' as a single item.
*   **Materials:** Large numeral '1' card, one toy panda (HK Ocean Park symbol), one piece of toy dim sum (e.g., siu mai).
*   **Procedure:**
    1.  **Introduce Numeral:** "Look! This is number **one**! Can you show me **one** finger?" (Children show one finger).
    2.  **Counting Objects:** "Today, we have a special visitor from Ocean Park! It's **one** panda! Let's say 'one panda'." (Hold up the toy panda).
    3.  **HK Cultural Connection:** "Who likes yummy dim sum? I have **one** siu mai! Just one! Can you say 'one siu mai'?" (Show one toy siu mai).
    4.  **Hands-on:** "Now, everyone gets **one** sticker! Put your one sticker on your shirt!" (Give each child one sticker).
    5.  **Reinforcement:** Ask children to pick up "one block" or "one crayon" from a tray.

---

**Activity 2: Number 2 - "Two Friends"**

*   **Concept:** Understanding 'two' as a pair or two separate items.
*   **Materials:** Large numeral '2' card, two toy double-decker buses, two pairs of chopsticks, two blocks.
*   **Procedure:**
    1.  **Introduce Numeral:** "Wow, look! This is number **two**! Can you show me **two** fingers?" (Children show two fingers).
    2.  **Counting Objects (HK Connection):** "Look at these amazing Hong Kong buses! Let's count them: **one, two**! Two buses! Our buses always go together, like two good friends!"
    3.  **Daily Object Counting:** "What about our hands? We have **two** hands! (Wiggle both hands). And our feet? We have **two** feet!"
    4.  **Hands-on:** "In Hong Kong, we use chopsticks to eat! I have two chopsticks here. Can you find **two** blocks and put them in the basket? Count them as you put them in: **one, two**."

---

**Activity 3: Number 3 - "Three Cheers!"**

*   **Concept:** Understanding 'three' as a group of three.
*   **Materials:** Large numeral '3' card, three toy goldfish (common pets/symbols in HK), three Play-Doh balls, three plastic bowls.
*   **Procedure:**
    1.  **Introduce Numeral:** "Hello, number **three**! Can you show me three fingers?" (Children show three fingers).
    2.  **Action Counting:** "Let's do three big jumps! **One, two, three!** And three claps! **One, two, three!**"
    3.  **Counting Objects (HK Connection):** "These are beautiful goldfish! How many do we have? Let's count carefully: **one, two, three**! Three goldfish swimming!"
    4.  **Hands-on:** "Today we are making yummy HK egg tarts with Play-Doh! Can you take **three** Play-Doh balls and put them in your bowl? Count them as you go: **one, two, three**."
    5.  **Reinforcement:** Have children build a tower with "three" blocks, counting each block as they stack.

---

**Consolidation & Review (5 minutes): "What's My Number?" Game**

*   **Teacher:** "Let's play 'What's My Number?' I will hold up some objects, and you tell me how many!"
*   **Procedure:**
    1.  Hold up **one** object (e.g., a crayon). "How many crayons?" (Children say "one!").
    2.  Hold up **two** objects (e.g., two small books). "How many books?" (Children say "two!").
    3.  Hold up **three** objects (e.g., three small balls). "How many balls?" (Children say "three!").
    4.  **Song:** Finish with "One, Two, Three, Let's Count With Me!" song again.

    4.  **Hands-on:** Provide a tray of small objects. "Can you put **four** buttons into this section? Count them as you put them down."

**Activity 5: Number 5 - "Five Little Friends"**

*   **Concept:** Understanding 'five' as a group of five, relating to a hand.
*   **Materials:** Large numeral '5' card, five small toy monkeys, five toy cookies/biscuits, child's hand.
*   **Procedure:**
    1.  **Introduce Numeral:** "It's number **five**! Show me all the fingers on one hand! **One, two, three, four, five**! Five fingers!"
    2.  **Song Connection:** "Remember our 'Five Little Monkeys Jumping on the Bed' song? Let's count our five little monkeys: **one, two, three, four, five**!" (Use five toy monkeys).
    3.  **Hands-on (HK Snack):** "Yummy cookies! Who wants five cookies? Let's count them: **one, two, three, four, five**." (Give each child five toy cookies to count and 'eat').
    4.  **Tracing:** Provide number '5' tracing sheets or sand trays for children to trace the numeral.

**Activity 6: Number 6 - "Six Rolling Dice"**

*   **Concept:** Understanding 'six' and recognizing dot patterns.
*   **Materials:** Large numeral '6' card, dice with dots, six colourful blocks, six plastic bowls.
*   **Procedure:**
    1.  **Introduce Numeral:** "This is number **six**! Can you show me five fingers on one hand, and one more on the other? That's six!"
    2.  **Counting Objects:** "Look at this dice! Sometimes it shows **six** dots! Can you find the side with six dots?" (Help children identify).
    3.  **Hands-on:** "Let's give each bowl six colourful blocks. Count them carefully: **one, two, three, four, five, six**." (Children count and place blocks).
    4.  **HK Cultural (simplified):** "Sometimes in Hong Kong, we see a mini-bus with six seats inside! Let's pretend to count six friends getting on our mini-bus: **one, two, three, four, five, six**!" (Children pretend to count friends getting on a pretend bus).

**Consolidation & Review (5 minutes): "Number Hunt"**

*   **Teacher:** "Let's go on a number hunt! Can you find a group of **one** item? How about **two** items? **Three**? **Four**? **Five**? **Six**?"
*   **Procedure:**
    1.  Hide number cards 1-6 around the room.
    2.  Call out a number (e.g., "Find number 4!"). Children find the card.
    3.  Ask them to find groups of objects corresponding to the numbers they've learned (e.g., "Can you find two chairs?", "Find three books").

**Activity 10: Number 10 - "Ten Little Toes"**

*   **Concept:** Understanding 'ten' as a complete set of ten, relating to fingers/toes.
*   **Materials:** Large numeral '10' card, ten small plastic coins (like HK cents), ten small toy dim sum pieces (variety).
*   **Procedure:**
    1.  **Introduce Numeral:** "Hooray! It's number **ten**! Show me all your fingers! **One, two, three, four, five, six, seven, eight, nine, ten**! Ten fingers!"
    2.  **Counting Body Parts:** "We have ten fingers and ten toes! Wiggle your ten fingers, wiggle your ten toes!"
    3.  **Hands-on (HK Connection):** "Let's pretend we're buying dim sum! Here are ten HK coins! Count them: **one, two, three, four, five, six, seven, eight, nine, ten**! Now, use your coins to 'buy' ten yummy dim sum pieces! Let's count the dim sum: **one, two, three... ten**!" (Children count out ten toy dim sum pieces).
    4.  **Activity:** "Ten-Block Train!" Provide ten blocks. "Can you make a super long train with ten blocks? Count them all!"

**Consolidation & Review (10 minutes): "Big Number Review!"**

*   **Teacher:** "We've learned so many numbers! Let's see if we can find them!"
*   **Procedure:**
    1.  **Number Line Walk:** Have a large number line (1-10) on the floor. Call out a number (e.g., "Jump to number 5!"), and children jump to it.
    2.  **Counting Story:** Read a simple picture book that features counting from 1 to 10. (e.g., "Counting with the Very Hungry Caterpillar").
    3.  **"How Many?" Game:** Hold up varying numbers of objects (1-10). Ask "How many?" and encourage children to count aloud.
    4.  **Celebration:** Sing the "Ten Little Fingers" song one last time, celebrating their achievement in learning to count to 10!

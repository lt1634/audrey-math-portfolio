Stage 4: Advanced Concepts

Multiplication/Division for 6-Year-Olds

1

Basic Multiplication

Children understand multiplication as repeated addition and grouping objects into equal sets.

Example: 3 groups of 2 apples = 6 apples (3 × 2 = 6)
2

Basic Division

Children understand division as sharing equally and separating objects into equal groups.

Example: 12 cookies shared among 3 friends = 4 cookies each (12 ÷ 3 = 4)
3

Basic Fractions

Children learn to recognize and work with simple fractions like halves, thirds, and quarters.

Example: Cutting a pizza into halves or sharing a chocolate bar equally
4

Number Patterns

Children recognize and create patterns in numbers, shapes, and sequences.

Example: 2, 4, 6, 8... or red, blue, red, blue...
5

Basic Measurement

Children learn to compare sizes, lengths, and quantities using non-standard units.

Example: Measuring with hands, feet, or blocks

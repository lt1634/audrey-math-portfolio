Stage 5: Geometry

Shapes and Measurements for 7-Year-Olds

1

Shape Recognition

Children can identify and name basic geometric shapes in 2D and 3D forms.

Example: Circle, square, triangle, rectangle, cube, sphere
2

Basic Measurements

Children learn to measure length, width, height using standard and non-standard units.

Example: Using rulers, measuring tapes, and comparing sizes
3

Spatial Reasoning

Children develop understanding of spatial relationships, directions, and 3D construction.

Example: Building with blocks, understanding above/below, left/right
4

Shape Properties

Children learn about sides, angles, vertices, and how shapes can be combined or transformed.

Example: Counting sides of polygons, identifying right angles
5

Area and Perimeter

Children begin to understand concepts of area and perimeter through hands-on activities.

Example: Counting squares to find area, measuring around shapes
